Figure (a) shows the difference between 3D Gaussian and Cauchy distributions. Note that the Cauchy distribution is generated in its standard form, then rescaled by applying the Gaussian's covariance matrix.. \[\] It is easy to show the Cauchy distribution does not have a well-defined variance (or indeed any moments): \[\int_{-\infty}^\infty dx~x^2p(x) =\int_{-\infty}^\infty dx~\frac{x^2}{1+x^2} \] Note the integrand tends to \(1\) as \(x\rightarrow \infty\), ensuring the integral diverges.
